Case Studies

Welcome to your financial literacy journey! These lessons are designed to help you understand how money works, from budgeting and saving to investing. Learning these skills now will set you up for a successful future, giving you the tools to make smart financial decisions and reach your goals. Let's dive in and start mastering your money!

Case Study 1: Start Investing in Stocks

Jake, a high school student, has saved $300 and wants to start investing in the stock market. His friend recommends he invest in a highly risky technology company, but Jake wants to diversify his portfolio. He split his money into three different stocks—$100 in a highly risky technology stock, $100 in a stable healthcare stock and $100 in a moderately volatile retail stock. Over six months, the technology stock grows by 10%, healthcare stock stays the same, while the retail stock drops by 5%. Jake is unsure whether he made the right choice.

A) Jake should have invested all his money in a highly risky technology stock.
B) Jake should not have invested in stocks at all.
C) Jake made the right choice to diversify his portfolio.
D) Jake should have invested all his money in the stable healthcare stock.

Case Study 2: Mutual Funds vs. Individual Stocks

Lily has $1,000 to invest, and she’s torn between investing in individual stocks or a mutual fund. Her older brother explains that a mutual fund pools money from different investors and invests in a diverse range of assets, offering lower risk. Lily does some research and finds that while stocks can provide high returns, they also come with more risk. A mutual fund she is considering has a projected annual return of 6%, while a popular stock she’s eyeing has shown average returns of 12%, but with higher volatility. She must decide where to invest her money. Which investment is likely to offer Lily lower risk but stable returns?

A) Individual stocks
B) Mutual funds
C) Real estate
D) Bonds

Case Study 3: Long-Term Growth with ETFs

Carlos is interested in Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) as a beginner investor. He learns that ETFs are similar to mutual funds but are traded on stock exchanges like individual stocks. Carlos has $500 to invest and chooses an ETF that tracks the overall stock market. Historically, this ETF has grown at an average annual rate of 7%. Carlos plans to leave his money invested for five years and doesn’t plan to touch it during that time. Carlos wonders what's the primary advantage of investing in an ETF over individual stocks?

A) ETFs have a higher risk than individual stock
B) ETFs have a lower risk due to diversification
C) ETFs have guaranteed returns
D) ETFs have no risk

Case Study 4: Dividend Stocks

Anna is interested in investing in dividend stocks, which pay regular cash dividends to shareholders. She invests $400 in a utility company that pays a 3% annual dividend. In addition, the stock itself appreciates by 5% over the course of a year. Anna plans to reinvest her dividends, allowing her to buy more shares of the stock each year. After one year, she wants to calculate how much her total investment has grown, including both stock appreciation and the dividend reinvestment. What is Anna’s total return after one year, considering both dividends and stock appreciation?

A) $32
B) $38
C) $40
D) $42

Case Study 5: Risk Tolerance and Cryptocurrency
Sophia's parents give $500 to her to pay her college fees, which is due in 3 months. Sophia's friend suggests her to invest in cryptocurrency for these 3 months because of the potential for high returns. She learns, however, that cryptocurrencies can be very volatile, with prices rising or falling rapidly. A few months ago, one popular cryptocurrency rose by 50% in just a week, but the next week it dropped by 30%. Sophia understands that while she could make a lot of money quickly, she could also lose a significant portion of her investment. Sophia needs to pay her college fees in 3 months. She considers her risk tolerance and wonders if this type of investment is right for her.
A) Sophia can earn significant returns on the cryptocurrency within 3 months and so she should invest in cryptocurrency.
B) Cryptocurrency investments are safe investments and she will not lose the principal if she invests.
C) Sophia should invest in cryptocurrency since her friend advised her to.
D) Sophia should not invest in cryptocurrency since it is a very volatile type of investment and she needs the money in 3 months.
