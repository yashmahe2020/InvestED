Quiz - Live Below Your Means

LET'S PRACTICE WHAT YOU LEARNED!

Question 1: What is a "need"?
A) Something you want because everyone else has it
B) Something essential for your survival and well-being
C) Something you can live without
D) Something you only buy for fun

Question 2: Which of the following is an example of a "want"?
A) Paying for your school lunch
B) Buying medicine when you’re sick
C) Paying rent or a mortgage
D) A new smartphone when your old one works fine

Question 3: What does "living below your means" mean?
A) Spending more than you earn
B) Saving a small portion of your income
C) Spending less than you earn and saving the rest
D) Using credit cards to buy things you can’t afford

Question 4: What’s a good way to avoid overspending?
A) Buy everything that’s on sale
B) Use your credit card for all purchases
C) Make a budget and stick to it
D) Spend more money than you have

Question 5: How does living below your means help you build wealth?
A) It makes sure you can buy everything you want immediately
B) It allows you to save more money for the future and invest wisely
C) It doesn’t affect your financial future at all
D) It allows you to spend as much money as you want without any worry
